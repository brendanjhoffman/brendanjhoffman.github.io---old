Transferring games on a local network.

The gaming one.


Look, you don't want to redownload a 12 gigabyte game and wait three hours, then wait another three for your roommate to download it. You don't want to download it at the same time and take eight hours.

  • Let's call the one who already downloaded a game the host, and the one who needs it the client.
  • Host: make sure the folder that permission to be shared. Right click the folder you want to share and go to Properties > Sharing > Share.
  • Host: Press the Win+R and type "cmd", press enter. Take note of your name here: C:\Users\USERNAME>. Type "ipconfig". You should see a line "IPv4 Address. . . : 192.168.X.XXX". Take note of that, too.
  • Client: Open explorer with Win+E. In the top bar (it'll say something like Quick Access). Paste in \\192.168.X.XXX.
  • Host: You'll have to log in to give the client access. Use your username from step 2 and your password that you use to log in.
  • Client: Find the game you want. It'll probably be in something like X:\Steam\steamapps\common\GameName.
  • Client: Copy and paste or drag and drop it to your Steam common folder, usually of similar location.
