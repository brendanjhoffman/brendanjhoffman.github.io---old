B2B eCommerce platform for pharmacies

The collaborative one.

Did you know the Rx ( â„ž ) symbol is derived from the Latin word for "recipe" or to "take?" That's the origin of our team, "Team Latin For Recipe." Our sponsor, Amantha, came to UCI's Informatics department with a proposal: build an online buyer-to-buyer marketplace for independent pharmacies.

I was lucky to partner up with Khoi, Alan, Yuya, Garry, and Josh to build RxPost.

The Problem:
Pharmacies buy in bulk from wholesalers, and often have their drugs expire before they can sell. RxPost lets pharmacies buy and sell excess products, saving both parties money.

The Solution:
I was excited for this project since displaying and manipulating an asynchronous database was nothing new to me (see PMSNC). We used a LAMP stack deployed on AWS. Although we all had weaknesses, we found our strengths. Khoi had experience with the PayPal API, Yuya was great at mockup design, Josh, Alan, and Garry worked on the front end, and I worked on writing the SQL calls in PHP, as well as managing our AWS EC2 instance.
